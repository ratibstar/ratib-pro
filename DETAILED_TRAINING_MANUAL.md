## 🚀 Getting Started - First Login

### Task: Logging Into the System

**Step 1:** Open your web browser (Chrome, Firefox, or Edge recommended)

**Step 2:** In the address bar at the top of your browser, type the URL provided by your administrator
- Example: `https://out.ratib.sa` or `http://localhost/ratibprogram`
- Press **Enter** on your keyboard

**Step 3:** Wait for the login page to load completely
- You will see a login form with:
  - A username field (usually the first field)
  - A password field (usually below the username)
  - A "Login" button
  - Possibly a "Forgot Password?" link

**Step 4:** Click inside the **username field** (the first empty box)
- The cursor will appear inside the field
- The field border may highlight

**Step 5:** Type your username exactly as provided by your administrator
- Make sure Caps Lock is OFF (unless your username requires capital letters)
- Type carefully - usernames are case-sensitive

**Step 6:** Press the **Tab** key on your keyboard OR click inside the **password field**
- This moves your cursor to the password field

**Step 7:** Type your password
- Passwords are hidden (you'll see dots or asterisks instead of letters)
- Type carefully - passwords are case-sensitive

**Step 8:** Click the **"Login"** button
- The button is usually green or blue
- Located below the password field
- You can also press **Enter** on your keyboard

**Step 9:** Wait for the system to process your login
- You may see a loading indicator
- Do NOT click the button multiple times

**Step 10:** If login is successful:
- You will be automatically redirected to the **Dashboard**
- You will see a welcome message or your name at the top
- The left sidebar menu will appear

**Step 11:** If login fails:
- You will see an error message (usually in red)
- Common messages:
  - "Invalid username or password"
  - "Account is disabled"
  - "Please check your credentials"
- Try again with correct credentials
- If you forgot your password, click "Forgot Password?" link

## 🏠 Understanding the Dashboard

### Task: Navigating the Dashboard

**Step 1:** After logging in, you will automatically see the **Dashboard**
- The Dashboard is your main overview page
- It shows statistics for all modules

**Step 2:** Look at the **left side** of your screen
- You will see a vertical menu (sidebar)
- This menu contains all available modules
- Each menu item has an icon and text

**Step 3:** Identify the menu items:
- **Dashboard** (🏠 icon) - You are here
- **Agent** (👥 icon) - Manage agents
- **SubAgent** (👤 icon) - Manage subagents
- **Workers** (🔧 icon) - Manage workers
- **Cases** (📋 icon) - Manage cases
- **Accounting** (💰 icon) - Financial management
- **HR** (👔 icon) - Human resources
- **Reports** (📊 icon) - View reports
- **Contact** (📞 icon) - Manage contacts
- **Notifications** (🔔 icon) - See alerts
- **Help & Learning Center** (📚 icon) - Get help
- **Logout** (🚪 icon) - Sign out

**Step 4:** Look at the **center/main area** of the Dashboard
- You will see **cards** (boxes) for each module
- Each card shows:
  - Module name (e.g., "Agents", "Workers")
  - Statistics (Total, Active, Inactive counts)
  - Status indicator (colored dot or text)

**Step 5:** Understanding the statistics cards:
- **Total** - Total number of records
- **Active** - Number of active records (usually green)
- **Inactive** - Number of inactive records (usually red or gray)

**Step 6:** To open a module:
- **Click** on any card (box) with your mouse
- OR click the corresponding menu item in the left sidebar
- The page will change to show that module's content

**Step 7:** Look at the **top right** of the screen
- You will see your name (if displayed)
- You may see a notification bell icon (🔔)
- You may see a profile icon (👤)

**Step 8:** To return to Dashboard:
- Click **"Dashboard"** in the left menu
- OR click the Dashboard card
- OR click the logo/company name at the top left

## 🔧 Workers Management - Complete Guide

### Task 1: Opening the Workers Page

**Step 1:** From the Dashboard, locate the **Workers card**
- Look for a card with "Workers" text and a 🔧 icon
- The card shows statistics: Total, Active, Inactive workers

**Step 2:** **Click** on the Workers card with your mouse
- OR click "Workers" in the left sidebar menu
- Wait for the page to load (you may see a loading spinner)

**Step 3:** Once loaded, you will see:
- **Top section:** Statistics cards showing worker counts
- **Middle section:** Search bar and filters
- **Bottom section:** Table showing all workers

### Task 2: Understanding the Workers Page Layout

**Step 1:** Look at the **top of the Workers page**
- You will see **statistics cards** showing:
  - Total Workers (with number)
  - Active Workers (with number, usually green)
  - Inactive Workers (with number, usually red)
  - Pending Workers (with number, usually yellow)
  - Suspended Workers (with number, usually orange)

**Step 2:** Below the statistics, find the **control buttons**:
- **"Add New Worker"** button (usually green, with + icon)
- This button is on the left side
- Below it, you may see bulk action buttons (if workers are selected)

**Step 3:** On the **right side**, find:
- **Search box** - A text input field with a search icon (🔍)
- **Status filter dropdown** - A dropdown menu showing "All Status"

**Step 4:** Below the controls, you will see the **workers table**
- The table has columns:
  - Checkbox column (for selecting workers)
  - ID column
  - Name column
  - Passport Number column
  - Nationality column
  - Agent column
  - Status column (with colored indicators)
  - Actions column (with buttons: View, Edit, Delete)

### Task 3: Adding a New Worker - Complete Step-by-Step

**Step 1:** On the Workers page, locate the **"Add New Worker"** button
- The button is usually green
- It has a **+** (plus) icon
- It says "Add New Worker" or similar text

**Step 2:** **Click** the "Add New Worker" button
- A form will appear (usually as a popup or modal window)
- The form may cover part of the screen
- The form title says "Add New Worker"

**Step 3:** You will see a **sidebar navigation** on the left side of the form
- Sections available:
  - **Basic Info** (👤 icon) - Currently active (highlighted)
  - **Professional** (💼 icon)
  - **Contact** (📧 icon)
  - **Documents** (📄 icon)

**Step 4:** Start with **Basic Information** section (already open)

**Step 5:** Find the **"Full Name"** field
- It has a label "Full Name" with an asterisk (*) indicating it's required
- Click inside this field
- Type the worker's full name
- Example: "Ahmed Mohammed Ali"

**Step 6:** Find the **"Gender"** dropdown field
- Label says "Gender" with asterisk (*)
- Click on the dropdown arrow
- Select either "Male" or "Female"
- Click to select

**Step 7:** Find the **"Age"** field (if available)
- Click inside the field
- Type the worker's age as a number
- Example: "25"
- Only numbers allowed

**Step 8:** Find the **"Marital Status"** dropdown (if available)
- Click the dropdown
- Select: Single, Married, Divorced, or Widowed

**Step 9:** Find the **"Date of Birth"** field (if available)
- Click inside the field
- A calendar may appear
- Select the date OR type date in format: YYYY-MM-DD
- Example: "1998-05-15"

**Step 10:** Find the **"Nationality"** field
- Click inside the field
- Type the country name OR select from dropdown if available
- Example: "Egypt", "Bangladesh", "India"

**Step 11:** Find the **"Passport Number"** field
- Click inside the field
- Type the passport number exactly as it appears
- Example: "A12345678"
- This is usually required

**Step 12:** Find the **"Passport Issue Date"** field (if available)
- Click inside the field
- Select or type the date when passport was issued

**Step 13:** Find the **"Passport Expiry Date"** field (if available)
- Click inside the field
- Select or type the date when passport expires

**Step 14:** Click on **"Professional"** section in the sidebar (💼 icon)
- The form will switch to Professional Details section

**Step 15:** Find the **"Agent"** dropdown
- This is usually required
- Click the dropdown arrow
- Select the agent from the list
- If no agents exist, you must create one first (see Agents section)

**Step 16:** Find the **"Subagent"** dropdown (optional)
- Click the dropdown
- Select a subagent if applicable
- Can be left empty

**Step 17:** Find the **"Visa Type"** dropdown (if available)
- Click the dropdown
- Select the type of visa
- Example: "Work Visa", "Visit Visa"

**Step 18:** Find the **"Job Category"** field
- Click inside the field
- Type the job category OR select from dropdown
- Example: "Construction", "Domestic Worker", "Driver"

**Step 19:** Find the **"Salary"** field (if available)
- Click inside the field
- Type the salary amount as a number
- Example: "1500"
- Do not include currency symbols

**Step 20:** Find the **"Status"** dropdown
- Click the dropdown
- Select initial status:
  - **Pending** - New worker, awaiting approval
  - **Active** - Approved and active
  - **Inactive** - Not currently active
- For new workers, usually select "Pending"

**Step 21:** Click on **"Contact"** section in the sidebar (📧 icon)

**Step 22:** Find the **"Phone Number"** field
- Click inside the field
- Type the phone number
- Include country code if needed
- Example: "+966501234567"

**Step 23:** Find the **"Email"** field (if available)
- Click inside the field
- Type the email address
- Example: "worker@example.com"
- Must be a valid email format

**Step 24:** Find the **"Address"** field (if available)
- Click inside the field
- Type the full address
- Can be multiple lines

**Step 25:** Click on **"Documents"** section in the sidebar (📄 icon)

**Step 26:** You will see document sections for:
- **Identity Document**
- **Passport**
- **Medical Certificate**
- **Police Clearance**
- **Visa**
- **Ticket**

**Step 27:** For each document type you want to upload:
- Find the document section (e.g., "Identity Document")
- Click the **"UPLOAD"** button next to that document type
- A file browser window will open
- Navigate to the file on your computer
- Select the file (PDF, JPG, PNG formats usually accepted)
- Click **"Open"** in the file browser
- Wait for upload to complete
- You will see the file name appear

**Step 28:** After filling all required fields:
- Scroll to the bottom of the form
- You will see two buttons:
  - **"Cancel"** button (usually gray or red)
  - **"Save Worker"** button (usually green or blue)

**Step 29:** **Click** the **"Save Worker"** button
- Wait for the system to process
- You may see a loading indicator

**Step 30:** If successful:
- A success message will appear (usually green)
- The form will close automatically
- The new worker will appear in the workers table
- You may see a message like "Worker added successfully"

**Step 31:** If there are errors:
- Error messages will appear (usually red)
- Common errors:
  - "Full Name is required"
  - "Passport Number already exists"
  - "Please select an Agent"
- Fix the errors and click "Save Worker" again

### Task 4: Searching for a Worker

**Step 1:** On the Workers page, locate the **search box**
- It's usually on the right side, near the top
- Has a search icon (🔍) inside or next to it
- Placeholder text says "Search by ID, name, email..." or similar

**Step 2:** **Click** inside the search box
- The cursor will appear
- The box may highlight

**Step 3:** Type your search term
- You can search by:
  - Worker name
  - Passport number
  - Worker ID
  - Email address
- Example: Type "Ahmed" to find workers named Ahmed

**Step 4:** As you type, the table will **automatically filter**
- Workers matching your search will appear
- Other workers will be hidden
- No need to press Enter

**Step 5:** To clear the search:
- **Delete** all text in the search box
- OR click the X icon if available
- All workers will appear again

### Task 5: Filtering Workers by Status

**Step 1:** On the Workers page, locate the **Status filter dropdown**
- It's usually next to the search box
- Currently shows "All Status"

**Step 2:** **Click** on the dropdown arrow
- A menu will appear with options:
  - All Status
  - Active
  - Inactive
  - Pending
  - Suspended
  - (Other statuses if available)

**Step 3:** **Click** on the status you want to filter by
- Example: Click "Active" to see only active workers

**Step 4:** The table will **immediately update**
- Only workers with that status will be shown
- Other workers will be hidden

**Step 5:** To see all workers again:
- Click the dropdown again
- Select "All Status"

### Task 6: Viewing Worker Details

**Step 1:** In the workers table, find the worker you want to view
- Use search or scroll to find them

**Step 2:** In the **Actions** column (rightmost column), find the **"View"** button
- It usually has an eye icon (👁️)
- OR says "View"

**Step 3:** **Click** the "View" button
- A detailed view window will open
- Shows all worker information:
  - Personal details
  - Professional information
  - Contact information
  - Documents list
  - Status history

**Step 4:** Scroll through the details to see all information

**Step 5:** To close the view:
- Click the **X** button (top right corner)
- OR click outside the window
- OR press **Esc** key on keyboard

### Task 7: Editing a Worker

**Step 1:** In the workers table, find the worker you want to edit
- Use search or scroll to find them

**Step 2:** In the **Actions** column, find the **"Edit"** button
- It usually has a pencil icon (✏️)
- OR says "Edit"

**Step 3:** **Click** the "Edit" button
- The worker form will open (same as Add form)
- All fields will be **pre-filled** with current worker data
- Form title says "Edit Worker" instead of "Add New Worker"

**Step 4:** Make your changes:
- Click on any field you want to change
- Edit the information
- You can navigate between sections using the sidebar

**Step 5:** After making changes:
- Scroll to the bottom
- Click **"Save Worker"** button (or "Update Worker")
- Wait for confirmation

**Step 6:** If successful:
- Success message appears
- Form closes
- Updated information appears in the table

### Task 8: Changing Worker Status

**Step 1:** Find the worker in the table

**Step 2:** Look at the **Status** column
- You will see the current status (e.g., "Pending", "Active")
- Status may be color-coded

**Step 3:** **Click** on the status text OR find the status dropdown in Actions column
- A dropdown menu may appear
- OR an edit form opens

**Step 4:** Select the new status from the dropdown:
- **Pending** - Awaiting approval
- **Active** - Approved and active
- **Inactive** - Not active
- **Suspended** - Temporarily suspended
- **Deployed** - Currently deployed
- **Returned** - Has returned

**Step 5:** **Click** "Update" or "Save" button
- Status changes immediately
- Color indicator updates

### Task 9: Uploading Documents to an Existing Worker

**Step 1:** Open the worker for editing (see Task 7)

**Step 2:** Click on **"Documents"** section in the sidebar (📄 icon)

**Step 3:** Find the document type you want to upload
- Example: "Medical Certificate"

**Step 4:** In that document section, find the **"UPLOAD"** button
- Click the button

**Step 5:** File browser opens:
- Navigate to the file location on your computer
- Select the file (PDF, JPG, PNG)
- Click **"Open"**

**Step 6:** Wait for upload to complete
- You'll see a progress indicator
- File name appears when done

**Step 7:** Fill in related fields if available:
- Document number
- Issue date
- Expiry date

**Step 8:** Click **"Save Worker"** at the bottom
- Document is saved with the worker record

### Task 10: Deleting a Worker

**Step 1:** Find the worker in the table

**Step 2:** In the **Actions** column, find the **"Delete"** button
- Usually has a trash icon (🗑️)
- May be red colored

**Step 3:** **Click** the "Delete" button
- A confirmation dialog will appear
- Message: "Are you sure you want to delete this worker?"

**Step 4:** **Click** "Yes" or "Delete" to confirm
- OR click "Cancel" to cancel

**Step 5:** If confirmed:
- Worker is deleted
- Success message appears
- Worker disappears from table

**⚠️ Warning:** Deletion is usually permanent. Make sure you want to delete before confirming.

### Task 11: Bulk Operations (Selecting Multiple Workers)

**Step 1:** In the workers table, find the **checkbox column** (leftmost column)
- Each row has a checkbox

**Step 2:** **Click** the checkbox next to each worker you want to select
- Checkbox will be checked (✓)
- Row may highlight

**Step 3:** Select multiple workers:
- Click checkboxes for all workers you want to include
- You can select as many as needed

**Step 4:** After selecting workers:
- Bulk action buttons become active (no longer grayed out)
- Buttons available:
  - **Activate** - Set all selected to Active
  - **Deactivate** - Set all selected to Inactive
  - **Pending** - Set all selected to Pending
  - **Suspended** - Set all selected to Suspended
  - **Delete** - Delete all selected

**Step 5:** **Click** the bulk action button you want
- Example: Click "Activate" to activate all selected workers

**Step 6:** Confirmation dialog appears
- Message: "Are you sure you want to [action] X workers?"
- X = number of selected workers

**Step 7:** **Click** "Yes" or "Confirm" to proceed
- Action is applied to all selected workers
- Success message appears

**Step 8:** To select all workers at once:
- Look for **"Select All"** checkbox in table header (if available)
- Click it to select/deselect all

### Task 1: Opening Accounting Module

**Step 1:** From Dashboard, **Click** **"Accounting"** in left menu (💰 icon)
- OR click Accounting card

**Step 2:** Accounting page loads
- You will see **tabs** at the top:
  - Control Panel (Dashboard)
  - Chart of Accounts
  - Journal Entries
  - Invoices
  - Bills
  - Banking & Cash
  - Financial Reports
  - And more...

### Task 2: Understanding Accounting Tabs

**Step 1:** **Click** on **"Control Panel"** tab (first tab)
- Shows overview dashboard
- Displays:
  - Total Invoices
  - Total Bills
  - Bank Balances
  - Accounts Receivable
  - Accounts Payable
  - Charts and graphs

### Task 3: Chart of Accounts

**Step 1:** **Click** **"Chart of Accounts"** tab

**Step 2:** You will see a tree structure of accounts:
- **Assets** - What you own
- **Liabilities** - What you owe
- **Equity** - Your investment
- **Income** - Money coming in
- **Expenses** - Money going out

**Step 3:** To add a new account:
- **Click** **"Add Account"** button
- Fill in:
  - Account Name (required)
  - Account Code (required)
  - Account Type (dropdown)
  - Parent Account (if sub-account)
- **Click** **"Save"**

**Step 4:** To edit an account:
- **Click** account name OR **Click** Edit icon
- Make changes
- **Click** **"Save"**

### Task 4: Creating a Journal Entry

**Step 1:** **Click** **"Journal Entries"** tab

**Step 2:** **Click** **"New Journal Entry"** button

**Step 3:** Form opens with fields:

**Step 4:** Fill in **"Date"** field
- Click date field
- Select date from calendar OR type: YYYY-MM-DD

**Step 5:** Fill in **"Reference Number"** (if available)
- System may auto-generate
- OR type your reference

**Step 6:** Fill in **"Description"** field
- Type what this entry is for
- Example: "Payment for office supplies"

**Step 7:** Add **Line Items**:
- **Line 1:**
  - **Click** Account dropdown - Select account
  - **Click** Debit field - Enter amount (e.g., "1000")
  - Leave Credit empty
  - Add description
  
- **Line 2:**
  - **Click** Account dropdown - Select different account
  - Leave Debit empty
  - **Click** Credit field - Enter SAME amount (e.g., "1000")
  - Add description

**Step 8:** **IMPORTANT:** Ensure **Total Debits = Total Credits**
- System will show totals
- If not equal, you'll see an error
- Adjust amounts until balanced

**Step 9:** **Click** **"Save"** or **"Post Entry"** button
- Entry is saved
- Success message appears

### Task 5: Creating an Invoice (Receivable)

**Step 1:** **Click** **"Invoices"** tab

**Step 2:** **Click** **"New Invoice"** button

**Step 3:** Fill in invoice form:

**Step 4:** Select **"Customer"** dropdown

- If customer doesn't exist, create one first

**Step 5:** Fill in **"Invoice Date"**
- Select today's date or invoice date

**Step 6:** Fill in **"Due Date"**

**Step 7:** Fill in **"Invoice Number"**

**Step 8:** Add **Invoice Items**:

- Fill in:
  - Description (e.g., "Consulting Services")
  - Quantity (e.g., "10")
  - Unit Price (e.g., "100")
  - Total calculates automatically
- Add more items if needed

**Step 9:** Review **Total Amount**
- System calculates total
- Check if correct

**Step 10:** Add **Notes** (optional)
- Type any additional notes

**Step 11:** **Click** **"Save Invoice"** button
- Invoice created
- Appears in Invoices list
- Customer now owes this amount

### Task 6: Creating a Bill (Payable)

### Task 7: Managing Bank Accounts

**Step 1:** **Click** **"Banking & Cash"** tab
